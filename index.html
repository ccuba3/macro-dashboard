Japan's Nikkei 225 rises to record high as Trump extends Iran ceasefire
Geopolitical tensions and rising yields pressure equities as Iran ceasefire extension provides fragile relief amid stagflationary crosscurrents.

Markets sold off broadly today with the S&P 500 dropping 0.63% and the Russell 2000 underperforming at -1.00%, as rising long-end Treasury yields (10Y at 4.29%, up ~1%) weighed on rate-sensitive sectors. Real estate (-1.93%), utilities (-1.75%), and industrials (-1.41%) led the decline, while energy (+1.45%) was the lone bright spot amid elevated oil prices. The VIX climbed to 19.50, reflecting building unease despite Trump's extension of the Iran ceasefire, which helped Japan's Nikkei hit a record high overnight.

The macro backdrop is increasingly stagflationary: CPI remains elevated with the index at 330.3, unemployment has crept up to 4.3%, and WTI crude sits above $100/bbl even as the spot market trades near $89. The 10Y-2Y spread has normalized to +52bps, confirming the curve re-steepening trade, but consumer sentiment at a dismal 56.6 signals households are under significant pressure. The Swiss National Bank's aggressive 50bp cut to 0.5% underscores global central bank divergence, with the dollar strengthening modestly (DXY 98.39) as European growth falters—Germany's services PMI slipped below 50.

The Warsh Fed nomination hearing keeping his 'regime-change' plan intact adds a significant layer of institutional uncertainty for monetary policy. With the Fed Funds rate at 3.64% and the 30Y mortgage rate at 6.3%, the housing market remains constrained despite 1.487M starts. Gold holding near $4,777 and Bitcoin rallying 2.25% to $77,576 suggest persistent demand for alternative stores of value amid fiscal and geopolitical uncertainty, while the potential $60B SpaceX-Cursor deal signals continued mega-cap tech consolidation.

⚠ Biggest Risk Today
An escalation in Hormuz Strait tensions or a breakdown in the Iran ceasefire could spike oil well above $100, triggering a full stagflationary shock with CPI re-accelerating and consumer spending collapsing.
📍 Most Sensitive Sector
Energy, because it sits at the nexus of Iran ceasefire developments, Hormuz Strait risk, and elevated crude prices that are simultaneously boosting sector earnings while threatening to crush the broader consumer economy.
