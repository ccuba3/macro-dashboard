Oil surges past $99 on Iran sanctions and UAE's OPEC exit as tech sells off and bond markets flash warning signs amid stagflationary crosscurrents.

Energy markets dominated today's macro narrative as WTI crude surged 3.2% to $99.48/bbl following new Treasury sanctions on Iranian oil exports and the UAE's shock exit from OPEC, which raises fundamental questions about the cartel's ability to manage supply. Wheat also spiked nearly 6%, reinforcing a broader commodity inflation impulse that sits uncomfortably alongside an already-elevated CPI of 330.3 and 10Y breakeven inflation at 2.44%. The energy supply shock is occurring against a backdrop where the Fed funds rate at 3.64% appears accommodative relative to the inflationary pressures building in the pipeline, and with the Fed meeting Wednesday, markets are bracing for a hawkish hold or guidance shift.

Equity markets reflected a clear rotation away from growth and risk toward defensives and real assets. The Nasdaq 100 dropped over 1% while the Russell 2000 fell 1.15%, even as financials, utilities, and real estate eked out gains. The 10Y Treasury yield rose to 4.35% while the 2Y held steady at 3.59%, steepening the curve to 52bps — a normalization that Jamie Dimon's warning about a looming 'bond crisis' from global debt accumulation makes particularly timely. Consumer sentiment at a dismal 53.3 with unemployment at 4.3% paints a picture of a consumer under pressure from tariff-inflated prices and now a potential gasoline price shock.

The Swiss National Bank's aggressive 50bp cut to 0.5% highlights the divergent global monetary policy landscape, with European manufacturing still deeply contractionary (German PMI at 43.2). The dollar firmed modestly to 98.60, though the weak DXY level and EUR/USD at 1.17 suggest structural dollar skepticism persists. Gold pulled back 1.5% to $4,607 in a likely profit-taking move, but the macro setup — geopolitical escalation, fiscal deficits, and central bank uncertainty — remains structurally supportive. Bitcoin's 1.5% decline mirrors the broader risk-off tone, confirming crypto continues to trade as a high-beta risk asset rather than a safe haven.

⚠ Biggest Risk Today
Oil breaking decisively above $100 on Iran conflict escalation and OPEC fragmentation would deliver a stagflationary shock that forces the Fed into an impossible choice between fighting inflation and supporting a weakening labor market.
📍 Most Sensitive Sector
Energy — the sector rallied 1.66% as crude approaches $100, and the combination of Iran sanctions, UAE's OPEC exit, and potential Middle East escalation makes energy the fulcrum for both inflation expectations and broader macro risk.
