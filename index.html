Risk-on rally broadens as tech earnings surge, gold hits record highs, and oil plunges on Iran deal hopes — but stagflation signals lurk beneath the surface.

Markets are flashing a peculiar mix of euphoria and caution today. Equities rallied broadly with the Russell 2000 leading at +1.75% and Nasdaq gaining 1.31%, driven by blockbuster earnings from AMD (+15%), Super Micro (+18%), and Samsung crossing $1 trillion in market cap on AI tailwinds. Yet gold's 3% surge to $4,656 and silver's 4% jump signal that investors are simultaneously hedging against monetary debasement and inflation persistence, a contradiction that cannot persist indefinitely.

The macro backdrop is increasingly stagflationary. CPI remains elevated, the Fed Funds rate sits at 3.64% with Fed officials publicly citing inflation concerns to defend hawkish dissents, while unemployment has crept up to 4.3% and consumer sentiment is deeply depressed at 53.3 — levels historically associated with recessionary conditions. The trade deficit widened in March and the administration is pushing forward with tariffs based on forced labor laws, adding further upward pressure on prices. Oil's 5.65% decline on Trump's pause of Strait of Hormuz operations and cited Iran deal progress provides temporary relief, but WTI at $100 remains a headwind for consumers already struggling with $6.30 mortgage rates.

The fixed income market tells a nuanced story: the 10Y-2Y spread has normalized to +50bps, the 10Y yield dipped to 4.42%, and long-duration bonds rallied modestly. However, the 2Y yield rising while the 10Y falls suggests the market is pricing in near-term policy tightness with longer-term growth concerns. The Swiss National Bank's aggressive 50bp cut to 0.5% and the weakening dollar index at 98.29 reflect a global easing impulse that the Fed cannot yet join, leaving the U.S. in a uniquely constrained policy position. M2 at $22.7 trillion remains ample, but it's flowing into hard assets and AI rather than the broader economy.

⚠ Biggest Risk Today
Stagflation crystallizes as persistent tariff-driven inflation forces the Fed to hold rates elevated while unemployment continues rising and consumer sentiment deteriorates further, eventually breaking the AI-driven equity rally.
📍 Most Sensitive Sector
Technology — the sector is carrying the entire market on AI earnings momentum (AMD, SMCI, Samsung), but at elevated valuations it is most vulnerable to any repricing of the rate path or demand slowdown.

PBOC Accommodative Easing
LATEST SIGNALS

USD/CNY at 6.82 shows a relatively stable and managed yuan, giving the PBOC room to maintain accommodative policy to support the domestic economy amid escalating U.S. tariff pressures. China hosting Iran's top diplomat ahead of Trump's visit signals active geopolitical maneuvering that could influence trade negotiations and currency policy.

MARKET INTERPRETATION

Markets expect continued PBOC easing through reserve requirement cuts and targeted lending facilities, with copper's 4.92% surge signaling expectations of Chinese stimulus-driven industrial demand. The PBOC is likely to tolerate modest yuan depreciation as a buffer against tariff impacts while avoiding destabilizing capital outflows.

YIELDS

Chinese government bond yields are expected to remain low as the PBOC maintains ample liquidity, with further RRR cuts likely keeping short-term rates suppressed.

EQUITIES

Accommodative PBOC policy and AI-sector momentum (evidenced by sanctioned Chinese firms competing on cheaper models) support Chinese tech and industrial equities despite trade headwinds.

USD

KEY RISK An escalation in U.S.-China tariff tensions during Trump's upcoming visit could force the PBOC into more aggressive easing that accelerates capital flight and destabilizes Asian currencies.
