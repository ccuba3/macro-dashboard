Strait of Hormuz blockade sends oil plunging on Iran deal hopes while equities rally and gold surges to record highs amid geopolitical uncertainty.

Markets are navigating a complex geopolitical landscape as the US blockade of Iran-linked ships at the Strait of Hormuz paradoxically pushes oil prices down nearly 9% on rising hopes for a negotiated resolution. WTI crude at $90.31 remains well below the $114 reference price embedded in macro data, suggesting the market is pricing in de-escalation rather than supply disruption. The S&P 500 pushed to 6,967, with tech and consumer discretionary leading the advance, while the VIX retreated nearly 4% to 18.36, signaling growing risk appetite despite headline risks.

The macro backdrop remains bifurcated: consumer sentiment at a dismal 56.6 and record gasoline price increases are squeezing household budgets, yet nonfarm payrolls at 158.6 million and robust retail sales of $738 billion suggest underlying economic resilience. The Fed Funds rate at 3.64% with the 2Y Treasury at 3.61% indicates the market sees the Fed near terminal, while the positive 10Y-2Y spread of 50 basis points and 10Y breakeven inflation at 2.37% suggest the bond market is cautiously optimistic on a soft landing. The Swiss National Bank's aggressive 50bp cut to 0.5% and weak German PMIs highlight diverging global monetary paths that are weighing on the dollar index at 98.11.

Gold's surge to $4,855 and silver's 5.3% rally underscore persistent safe-haven demand and central bank diversification away from the dollar, even as equities climb. The M2 money supply at $22.67 trillion continues to provide ample liquidity, supporting risk assets, but the 30Y mortgage rate at 6.37% and housing starts at 1.487 million suggest the housing sector is walking a tightrope. JPMorgan's earnings beat paired with Dimon's warning about 'increasingly complex' economic risks perfectly encapsulates the current environment: solid fundamentals overlaid with significant tail risks from the Middle East conflict, trade policy, and AI-driven disruption.

⚠ Biggest Risk Today
An escalation of the Strait of Hormuz blockade that fails to produce a diplomatic resolution could reverse the oil price decline, reignite inflation expectations, and force the Fed to hold rates higher for longer—crushing consumer sentiment and risk assets simultaneously.
📍 Most Sensitive Sector
Energy is the most sensitive sector because a nearly 9% single-day crude oil decline amid an active naval blockade creates extreme binary outcomes depending on whether Iran negotiations succeed or the conflict escalates.
