Risk-on rally broadens as yields fall and crude craters 11% on Iran war uncertainty, while gold soars past $4,800 and the Fed stays on hold.

Markets staged a broad-based rally with the Russell 2000 leading at +2.1%, signaling risk appetite is spreading beyond mega-cap tech despite a deeply uncertain macro backdrop. The 10-year Treasury yield fell to 4.25% as investors priced in a Fed on hold — Governor Waller explicitly cited the Iran conflict and labor market risks as reasons to pause. The 10Y-2Y spread at +55bps with a Fed Funds rate of 3.64% suggests the market expects the cutting cycle to resume eventually, but geopolitical fog is thick. The VIX at 17.48, down 2.5%, indicates complacency is creeping back even as the IMF warns the Middle East war will slow global growth.

The most dramatic move was crude oil's 11.3% collapse to $84 despite WTI spot at $100.72, suggesting futures are pricing in either a de-escalation scenario or severe demand destruction fears from the IMF's growth downgrade. Gold at $4,849 and silver surging nearly 3% tell a different story — one of persistent inflation hedging and safe-haven demand coexisting with the equity rally. The Swiss National Bank's aggressive 50bp cut to 0.5% and weak German PMI data underscore that the global economy outside the US is struggling, yet USD strength remains muted at 98.23, reflecting the Fed's dovish hold posture.

Corporate America is attempting to extend record profits by raising prices amid the Iran war, a dynamic that keeps core CPI sticky at elevated levels and complicates the Fed's path. Consumer sentiment at 56.6 remains deeply depressed, creating a dangerous divergence with equity market euphoria. The housing market shows resilience with starts at 1.487 million despite 6.3% mortgage rates, but the real test will be whether the war-driven supply shock in energy flows through to broader inflation. Tech's leadership — AMD, Oracle, Microsoft driving IGV higher — and Cerebras filing to IPO suggest AI enthusiasm remains the market's primary narrative engine.

⚠ Biggest Risk Today
A prolonged Iran conflict drives crude back above $110 and reignites inflation, forcing the Fed to hike rather than cut, collapsing the equity rally and tipping the already-fragile consumer into recession.
📍 Most Sensitive Sector
Energy — a stunning 2.76% decline despite an active Middle East war and $100+ WTI suggests the market is aggressively front-running either demand destruction or a ceasefire, making it the most binary bet on the board.
